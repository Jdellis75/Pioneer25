Isaiah 30:20
"Though Jehovah gives you bread in the form of distress and water in the form of oppression, your Grand Instructor will no longer hide himself, and you will see your Grand Instructor with your own eyes."
📖 Bible Writer & Date:
Written by Isaiah around 732 BCE. This verse is part of Jehovah’s message to rebellious Judah, offering hope even during discipline. — it-1, “Isaiah”
💡 Doctrinal Insight:
The phrase “Grand Instructor” emphasizes Jehovah’s role as a loving teacher even in times of hardship. He disciplines but doesn’t abandon. — w07 6/15 p. 7
🧠 Reflective Thought:
Even when life feels difficult, could Jehovah be teaching me something valuable? — Personal Meditation Guide
🌍 Modern Application:
Today, Jehovah uses the congregation and faithful teaching tools to help us “see” him clearly even amid trials. — w15 10/15 p. 7
🎓 Hebrew Nugget:
The Hebrew root of “Instructor” (yarah) also means “to direct or teach.” It’s the same root as “Torah.” — it-2 p. 1207
